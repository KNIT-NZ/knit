Engaging, trustworthy, and sustainable democracy requires modern digital infrastructure: open source public tools, cryptographic protocols, smart databases, and natural language interfaces.

By rapidly enabling the New Zealand Government to become a true digital-first system, we can:

- Bring our systems of governance into a genuinely shared space of understanding and ownership,

- Dramatically enhance the experience of every citizen’s interactions with agencies and services,

- Evolve the role of the Member of Parliament beyond performative constitutional minimalism, and

- Empower Ministers with new models of advice and information flow.

Legitimacy is a lagging indicator, and the best ideas are not conceived in committees, they are adopted after they've already proven inevitable. We are building a rapid experimental pathway towards a digital civic future that is independent of formal political adoption. ▍
