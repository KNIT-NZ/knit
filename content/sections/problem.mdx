part: Foundations
description: How centralised institutions lose pace, trust, and legitimacy.
order: 2
---

Decision-making is slow, centralised, and disconnected from citizens.

The problem is not merely that public institutions are inefficient. It is that their structures are often too intermittent, too opaque, and too blunt to express legitimate public agency in a fast-moving society.

When participation is reduced to occasional voting, democratic life becomes episodic rather than continuous.
